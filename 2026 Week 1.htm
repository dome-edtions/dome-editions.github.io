Anticipated Releases for 2026

 

Week 1 of 2026: Music Recap

With 2025 now in the rearview, it′s time to look forward to 2026!

Now seems like a better time than any to try to predict what to expect from this upcoming new year, at least, music-wise. With many artists beginning album rollouts near the end of 2025, I′d love to shout-out some upcoming releases I′m the most excited to hear in the coming months. Aside from that, I also want to make some predictions as to which artists will make comebacks this year, however unlikely they may seem.

Look, the premise is simple enough, so here is my list.

* * *

A$AP Rocky - Don′t Be Dumb

We are long overdue for some new A$AP Rocky music.

This time, it seems as though this release might be for real. With several album cover arts being revealed by Rocky himself (all of which were designed by Tim Burton), it′s seeming more like a close reality than a distant fantasy I′d often dreamed of.

The question remains: what will this album even sound like? Back in 2024, Rocky released 2 of my favorite hip-hop songs from that year, those being Highjack and Tailor Swif. They had this very vintage sound to them, kind of mimicking the early 2010s mixtape era Rocky emerged from initially. Still, it′s hard to know whether this album will actually sound like these singles, or better yet, whether these songs will even land on the final tracklist.

Who′s to say for sure? For all we know, this might just be another elaborate stunt that will never materialize. Fingers crossed that′s not the case.

* * *

Sarah Kinsley

Back in September 2025, Sarah Kinsley released the lead single Fleeting to her next album. Though I′d enjoyed some of the key songs from her 2024 debut album Escaper, none of them hit quite as hard as this latest track from her. There′s something supremely whimsical about the song across its colorful synth lead or Sarah′s vocals that are throwing it back to some of the art pop greats (I immediately think of Kate Bush when I hear these types of sounds).

Over the last few weeks, she has been increasingly teasing toward a new album to be released this year, though details are still trickling as I write this section. Most recently, the second single Lonely Touch can be expected as of next week, so you best believe I′ll keep an eye out for it.

* * *

Lexa Gates - I Am

Here′s an album that I′m eagerly looking forward to in the next two weeks.

If you′re not familiar with Lexa Gates by now, don′t worry. She′s been steadily releasing music within the 2020s, and I found out about her work through 2024′s Elite Vessel. Though I wasn′t blown away by the album back then (in fact, I remember describing it as ′sloshy′ at the time), it gradually grew on me over the last year. I even placed one of its songs on my year-end list of best tracks in 2024, so opinions do change.

Opinions change even faster when the music starts to click. Over the course of 2025, Lexa released 4 new singles during the leadup to this new album, and I′m happy to report that I′ve enjoyed every single one. Latency was a neat little jam that I listened to a lot during the summer, Past It features this really interesting chipmunk soul instrumental that sounds incredible, Nothing to Worry About highlights Lexa′s signature deep voice rap-singing, and most recently Estranged has one of the more unique song progressions she′s used so far.

All in all, I can definitely classify this among my top 5 most anticipated releases of the year!

* * *

Converge - Love Is Not Enough

As far as metal albums go, the new Converge release is easily the one I′m looking forward to the most in 2026!

To fans of the band, it really sounds like they′re operating at a level as intense and ferocious as ever, especially on the title track that they released late in 2025 to tease this full album. To me, it′s kind of obvious that this one is going to be a lock for a year-end list, which is why I′m so excited to actually inject this into my ears when the time comes. Sometimes, a little metalcore can go a long way.

* * *

RAYE

One artist who made considerable waves in 2025 was RAYE. Though she didn′t necessarily release much music at all, it′s been fascinating to see her transform into a global phenomenon thanks to her incredibly dynamic live performances backed by impressive jazz orchestrations.

Needless to say, she has found her style, and she is sticking to it on her latest single WHERE IS MY HUSBAND, a song that truly speaks to her most charismatic side. Not only is it an incredibly ambitious track on its own, but it′s seemingly opening up an album cycle that may or may not occur within 2026. Let′s hope for the former, shall we?

* * *

Doechii

At this point, Doechii isn′t an artist who requires an introduction. Her come-up story has been nothing short of inspiring to watch unfold, culminating in a Grammy victory for her 2024 mixtape Alligator Bites Never Heal. Even I was immediately swooned by the versatility she displayed on the project, with a lot of my highlights from it going on to become chart-topping hits within the last year.

However, skyrocketing to success so quickly came with a fair share of drawbacks, notably within the form of serial Internet complainers dismissing her talent as luck. Well, what a better way to snap back at her dissenters then with the surprising new track girl get up, a song released on December 30th that sounds like a victory lap and a warning call all at the same time.

And with a new album seemingly on the horizon, I′ll be there on day one to hear what else she has to say.

* * *

underscores

I mean, this is an obvious pick for me, but you should know how much I adore underscores′ music by now. If I haven′t made that evident through the innumerable references I′ve made to Wallsocket in the last few years, then let me repeat myself again.

Within the lane of modern pop music, no one is as exciting of a producer to me as April herself. She has a way of creating these super sticky melodic earworms unlike any other artist I could think of. You can hear evidence of this on her recent contributions to the new Danny Brown album, be it the super poppy Copycats or the much darker and more technical Baby.

This last track also seems to emulate a lot of the energy and sonic components featured in the latest underscores singles Music and Do It, both of which are kind of aligning in a way that feels very album-like. From the matching fonts in the cover arts to the similar sound style she is aiming for, I′m slowly but surely charging up my excitement for a full album sometime in 2026.

* * *

Gorillaz - The Mountain

Of course, another safe pick is the upcoming Gorillaz album. Entering this latest era with a handful of singles incorporating features the likes of Sparks, IDLES, and Yasiin Bey, the band is doing what they typically do best through these interesting genre combinations that seek to mold pop into something greater than its intended purpose.

While I did enjoy many tracks off the band′s 2023 album Cracker Island, I′d love to hear them take things back to a more refined conceptual direction like on their very classic Plastic Beach, an album I still hold in a high regard today as being their best. With the stacked feature list they presented, I′m sure it′s a tangible goal.

* * *

Joji - Piss in the Wind

An artist that we haven′t heard of in a while has to be Joji, who seems to be ramping up toward some big comeback. Over the course of 2025, he released several singles, most of them running quite a bit short time-wise.

I′ll be honest, most of these songs rang more like motifs than they did actual finished tracks, so I guess I′ll have to wait and see how well they work within an album context. If anything, I really loved the most recent single Love You Less above all the other ones. It has this really cool mix of ambient sounds and shoegaze aesthetics that makes me look forward to whatever Joji seems to be cooking this year.

* * *

Danger Mouse & Black Thought - Cheat Codes 2

Okay, maybe this one is a bit of a shot in the dark, but I do hope this prediction comes to fruition. In 2022, Danger Mouse & Black Thought collaborated to create one of my favorite hip-hop albums of the decade so far, that being Cheat Codes. It was such a wonderful combination of old-school boom bap with verses by some legendary rappers, and I still listen to it as often as it comes to mind.

With that being said, the duo released the song Up in 2025 which features a similar thematic branding and style to the previous album. Maybe I′m reaching a bit here, but I′d love for this single to be the catalyst to a potential sequel album.

* * *

Olivia Rodrigo - OR3

Again, this might be a bit of a reach, but I definitely wouldn′t mind a new Olivia Rodrigo album in 2026. It′s hard for me to ignore just how much Sour and Guts have grown on me over the last few years. I know I liked them at the time but, even lyrically, I′m just very impressed by the persona she built for herself through her music and style. With all that said, I have a feeling her next album might just be her best yet if she continues expanding creatively at the pace that she has so far.

* * *

Ladytron - Paradises

Maybe this is a band that′s gone a bit overlooked lately, but I′m still very much excited to hear this full album. I remember discovering Ladytron back in 2023 through their album Time′s Arrow, and that one proved to be a grower for me during that year. Already with the singles we′ve gotten, it′s sounding like a much headier and more thematic listen than I would′ve expected.

* * *

Thundercat

Perhaps he was just playing around with sounds in 2025, or maybe he was leading up to an album. It′s hard to tell for sure...

What I can say is that I′d love to hear I Wish I Didn′t Waste Your Time and Children of the Baked Potato land on an official Thundercat album. That latter song is especially zany with great vocal contributions by Remi Wolf, leading me to wish for even more eccentric collaborations on future tracks.

* * *

feeble little horse

Come on now! This Is Real is way too good of a song to not be a lead single for an upcoming album. Look, I know it released super early in 2025 and we haven′t had any updates from the band since then, but let me be optimistic for greatness. We can use more dreamers in this world.

* * *

Baby Keem

Speaking of dreams, I think it′s about time for a new Baby Keem album. I mean, I′m still hanging onto The Melodic Blue for dear life right now; I could really use a supplement of new material any day now. I′ll even take another Hillbillies with Kendrick if that′s what it′ll take!

* * *

My bloody valentine

And it′s pretty much at this point in the list where I get on my knees and start praying for artists to return. I know I′m not the only one who needs new MBV badly, but I think we need to collectively start begging to wish a new era of music into existence.

* * *

Frank Ocean

Please... Pretty please, I′ll take anything. He′s going to come back this year, I just know it. He has to come back... He just has to...

* * *

Basically...

...that′s the short list of artists I′ve compiled that I really want to hear more from in 2026. Obviously, I didn′t capture the entire scope of my favorite artists, but I narrowed it roughly to my most anticipated at the moment.

I suppose we′ll have to wait as 2026 unfolds to see how many of my predictions / copious wishes come true. Here′s to another great year for new music to discover!
